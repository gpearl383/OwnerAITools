The problem

Your phone is your best salesperson. Right now, it's ignoring customers.

Every missed call is a customer who already decided to hire someone. The only question is whether it's you.

You're on a job

Hands in a water heater, a patient in the chair, a crew to run. The phone rings, you can't grab it, and that $4,000 job calls the next name on Google. It happens every week — you just never see the invoice you lost.

Answering services bill you by the minute

$2–$5 a minute, and yes — they bill you for the spam calls too. The person answering is reading a script, knows nothing about your business, and takes a message you have to chase down anyway.

A receptionist covers 40 of 168 hours

Even a great one goes home at 5, takes lunch, gets sick, and takes vacations. The week has 168 hours, and emergencies love the other 128. Nobody's hiring a night shift for the front desk.

How it works

We build it. You forward your phone. That's it.

This is a done-for-you service, not software you have to learn. You never touch a dashboard.

1

We learn your business

One 45-minute call. Your services, your service area, your pricing, how you talk to customers. We script and build your receptionist, test it against real scenarios, and send you a number to call and hear it yourself before anything goes live.

2

Forward your phones

Keep your existing number — nothing changes for your customers. Smart forwarding sends calls to your receptionist always, after-hours only, or only when you don't pick up. Want out? Rollback is instant, any time.

3

Leads land in your inbox

Every call becomes an email: caller info, what they need, full transcript, and the recording. On higher tiers, appointments hit your calendar and contacts hit your CRM automatically. We review the calls and tune it every month.
